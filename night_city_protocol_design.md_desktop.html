# Design System Strategy: Night City Protocol (Desktop Evolution) ## 1. Overview & Creative North Star **Creative North Star: "Aggressive High-Fidelity"** The "Night City Protocol" design system is a high-octane, editorial interpretation of a "Dark Future" aesthetic. It moves away from the polite, rounded corners of modern web design, embracing a "Neon Brutalist" approach characterized by sharp angles, heavy borders, and a stark, high-contrast color palette. This desktop evolution focuses on expanding the "terminal" feel to larger displays, utilizing wide layouts and modular data blocks. ## 2. Visual Language & Principles * **Asymmetry & Tension:** Layouts should feel dynamic and slightly "unbalanced" to create a sense of urgency. Use overlapping elements and unexpected spacing. * **Data-Dense Aesthetic:** Interfaces should feel like functional terminals. Use technical labels, "status codes" (e.g., SYSTEM_READY, ENCRYPTED), and monospace-inspired typography to suggest a layer of underlying machine logic. * **Hard Edges:** Avoid rounded corners (radius: 0). Use diagonal cutouts (dog-ear corners) and thick, deliberate borders to reinforce a rugged, industrial feel. * **Glow & Chromatic Aberration:** Use subtle neon glows (box-shadows) and occasional color shifts to evoke a sense of digital distortion and high-energy power. ## 3. Color Palette (Cyber-Stark) * **Background (VOID):** `#000000` (Pure Black) or `#0A0A0A` (Deepest Charcoal). * **Primary Accent (PULSE_YELLOW):** `#FCF901` (or `#FFC700` for a warmer variant) — Use for high-priority CTAs, active states, and critical data points. * **Secondary Accent (NEON_CYAN):** `#00EEFC` — Use for technical labels, progress bars, and structural highlights. * **Tertiary Accent (SIGNAL_RED):** `#FF0044` — Reserved for errors, alerts, and destructive actions. * **Neutral (SILICON_GREY):** `#353535` / `#131313` — Use for borders, secondary text, and card backgrounds. ## 4. Typography (Space Grotesk) * **Typeface:** `Space Grotesk` (Google Fonts). It's a proportional sans-serif with a geometric, technical feel that balances readability with a sci-fi edge. * **Headlines:** Uppercase only, heavy weight (Bold/Black), tight tracking (tracking-tighter). * **Technical Labels:** Uppercase, monospace feel, small font size (10px-12px), wide tracking (tracking-widest). * **Body Copy:** Regular weight, clean, and legible. ## 5. Component Guidelines (Desktop) ### Navigation (The Matrix) * **Top Bar:** Minimal, fixed, with technical branding (e.g., `NC_PROTOCOL_V1.0`) and high-visibility action buttons (`TERMINAL_ACCESS`). * **Links:** Uppercase, tight tracking, with high-contrast hover states. ### Buttons (Tactical) * **Default:** Rectangular, sharp corners, bold uppercase text. * **Primary:** High-contrast background (Pulse Yellow) with black text. Often accompanied by a decorative "corner cut" or secondary underline. * **Interactions:** Use slight `translate` or `skew` on hover to simulate a physical, mechanical response. ### Cards (Modules) * **Structure:** Dark backgrounds (`#131313`) with subtle, high-contrast borders (1px or 2px). * **Accents:** Add "status bars" or "sector codes" (e.g., `CYBER_CORE`, `VOID_SECTOR`) at the top of cards. Use colored "glow" borders for active or highlighted states. ### Data Visualization * **Progress Bars:** Thin, high-contrast lines (Cyan or Yellow) against a dark track. Use for skill levels or system status. * **Counters:** Large, bold typography for statistics, often with technical labels above or below. ## 6. Iconography & Imagery * **Icons:** Use thin-stroke, geometric icons (Material Symbols Outlined). * **Imagery:** High-contrast, moody photography. Use tech-inspired textures (grids, code overlays) or map-like visualizations to integrate images into the design.
